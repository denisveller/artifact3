Place in Modernism| Место в Модернизме


Akhmatova was heavily connected to the modernist movement, though she continued to write poetry for over two decades after the end of the era. She was one of the founders of the Acmeist school of poetry, along with other famed Russian modernists such as Osip Mandelstam and Sergey Gorodetsky. Acmeism was a reactionary movement to the dominant school of poetry in Russia at the time, known as Symbolism. The Symbolists were known to use words to espouse highly romantic ideals and imagery, resulting in poetry that could be described as vague. Acmeism, taken from the greek “akme”(the best age of man or the time of flowering) sought to reject this notion. They would clear, precise words in order to elicit a very clear image. This movement was incredibly similar to another modernist movement in western Europe known as Imagism, which is more well known. In fact, Acmeism can probably be described as Russian Imagism.

As Akhmatova was so heavily involved with this movement that was unapologetically modernist, one must consider her to be a modernist. Though her style would evolve greatly over the coming years, and past the modernist age, her roots would remain firmly in Acmeism, even if she would take on grander, more widespread themes.
